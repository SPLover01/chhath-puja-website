About Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is a sacred Hindu festival dedicated to the Sun God and Chhathi Maiya, celebrated with devotion in Bihar, Jharkhand, and Uttar Pradesh. It involves fasting, prayers, and offerings by rivers or water bodies, symbolizing gratitude and purity.



Rituals of Chhath Puja

Nahay Khay


Day 1: Devotees take a holy bath and prepare simple meals to begin the fast.

Kharna


Day 2: A day-long fast ends with kheer and roti, shared with family.

Sandhya Arghya


Day 3: Offerings are made to the setting sun at a riverbank or water body.

Usha Arghya


Day 4: Final offerings to the rising sun, concluding the festival.
